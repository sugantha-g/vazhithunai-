The problem

Chennai Metro has admitted it: there's no real last-mile policy

Feeder buses and shuttles get added "based on demand and feasibility" — station by station, after commuters have usually already given up. Share autos fill the gap informally, but informally means unreliable.

  • Drivers decide, trip by trip, whether to stop for you at all.
  • Fares are negotiated on the spot — no meter, no standard rate.
  • A shared fare today just means total ÷ headcount — the person going 500m subsidises the one going 3km.
Why not just use Ola, Uber, or Namma Yatri?

Namma Yatri / Beckn fix driver-side discovery, not multi-rider matching or fair splitting.

Ola / Uber run closed dispatch built for single riders, not matched groups.

Vazhithunai adds the missing layer — direction-based matching and fair fare-splitting — on top of the open network these already run on.

How matching works

Pickup anywhere — priority near a metro or bus stop

Vazhithunai groups riders within a ~150–200m live radius who are heading the same bearing, then hands the confirmed group to a nearby Beckn-network driver — no new app for the driver to install.

The same government-mandated per-km rate auto meters are supposed to run on — locked in at match time using a live traffic-aware estimate, so no one faces a surprise mid-ride.

Fair, by design

You pay for your distance, not your headcount

  • Proportional split by actual drop distance — 500m and 3km are never priced the same.
  • Locked at match time, not renegotiated in traffic.
  • UPI-first, cash fully supported — your exact share is shown before you ride.
  • Detour surcharge, calculated the same way, paid straight to the driver.
